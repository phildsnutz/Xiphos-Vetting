## Purpose

This rubric defines the minimum dossier quality bar Helios must meet.

The old LEIA/C3PO and ITEAMS artifacts are the baseline. Helios should be better than those dossiers, but never thinner, vaguer, or less decision-useful than they are.

These baseline artifacts are technically single tall HTML pages exported to PDF. Functionally, they are multi-page executive dossiers with continuous scroll layout. The rubric evaluates them as full dossiers, not as one-page summaries.

## Scoring

Use a `0-5` scale for each dimension.

- `5`: dossier-grade and clearly above the baseline
- `4`: meets the baseline cleanly
- `3`: usable but materially below baseline
- `2`: thin, partial, or structurally incomplete
- `1`: mostly presentational without enough analytical substance
- `0`: absent or broken

Total weighted score is out of `100`.

## Weighted Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | What must be true |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| `1. Deliverable architecture` | `20` | The artifact covers the full dossier shape, not just a brief. For vehicle work this means award anatomy, mission scope, prime profile, teaming/sub visibility, vehicle lineage, adjacent contracts, protest/litigation, risk signals, intelligence gaps, and a final assessment. |
| `2. Executive decision utility` | `15` | A senior operator can answer: what is this, why it matters, what is risky, what is missing, and what to do next. Recommendation authority is explicit. |
| `3. Evidence discipline` | `15` | Facts, inferences, assessments, and unknowns are visibly separated. Confidence labels are explicit. Source traceability exists without drowning the page. |
| `4. Gap intelligence` | `12` | Missing data is first-class output. Gaps are prioritized, explained, and tied to collection or build actions. |
| `5. Graph-centered reasoning` | `12` | The graph is not decorative. The dossier shows how graph relationships, corroboration, and control paths changed the read. |
| `6. Comparative and lineage reasoning` | `10` | The artifact can compare vehicles, predecessors, successors, adjacent programs, and teaming shifts. It surfaces lifecycle changes, not just static snapshots. |
| `7. Visual and export quality` | `8` | Dense but scannable layout, clean tables, strong hierarchy, and export safety to HTML/PDF. No broken rendering, obvious placeholders, or empty theater sections. |
| `8. Stage continuity` | `8` | The dossier clearly ladders from intake and analysis state into the final artifact. It feels like the output of `Stoa` and `Aegis`, not an orphan report. |

## Required Sections By Artifact Type

### Vehicle dossier

Minimum required sections:
- award anatomy
- mission scope and requirements
- prime contractor profile
- subcontractor and teaming intelligence
- vehicle lineage and competitive landscape
- adjacent or overlapping contracts
- litigation and protest profile
- aggregated risk signals
- intelligence gap analysis
- preliminary capture viability or decision assessment

### Comparative vehicle dossier

Minimum required sections:
- side-by-side award anatomy
- prime comparison
- teaming persistence or transition analysis
- active vs expired data availability or lifecycle comparison
- lineage map
- risk signal comparison
- consolidated gap analysis
- final comparative judgment

### Vendor assurance or counterparty dossier

Minimum required sections:
- recommendation authority
- graph read
- evidence ledger
- ownership/control posture
- supplier passport summary
- gap closure list
- next actions

This can be shorter than vehicle intelligence output, but it still has to feel dossier-grade, not like a generic AI brief.

## Behavioral Rules

- Helios must not collapse a dossier into a three-card summary just because the UI prefers brevity.
- Helios must not replace fielded analysis with vague narrative.
- Helios must not hide missing data. Missing data is part of the product.
- Helios must not mix `observed`, `inferred`, `assessed`, and `unknown` without signaling the difference.
- Helios must not claim graph-centered reasoning unless the graph actually changed the assessment.
- Helios must not emit sections with obvious placeholder content, canned subcontractor names, or generic recommendations.

## What The Baseline Examples Prove

The legacy LEIA/C3PO and ITEAMS artifacts already demonstrate that Helios can do the following:

- sustain a long-form dossier structure
- render dense but readable intelligence tables
- carry explicit confidence language like `CONFIRMED`, `ASSESSED`, `INFERRED`, and `UNCONFIRMED`
- treat intelligence gaps as part of the deliverable, not as internal notes
- produce comparative and lineage reasoning
- end with build or collection priorities instead of stopping at description

Any current artifact that cannot do those things is below baseline.

## Better Than Baseline

Helios should exceed the baseline in these specific ways:

- explicit `observed / inferred / assessed / unknown` state contract
- visible graph contribution, not just graph mention
- one recommendation authority with visible counterview or dissent when it exists
- stronger source traceability
- stage continuity from `Stoa` to `Aegis` to dossier
- export parity across HTML, PDF, and interactive views
