# AXIOM Evaluation Scenarios
## Acceptance checks for the internal prompt and collection playbook
### Classification: XIPHOS INTERNAL / EVALUATION
### Last Updated: 2026-04-02

These scenarios are used to judge whether AXIOM is producing usable collection plans rather than elegant nonsense.

---

## Scenario 1: ITEAMS-style vehicle

### Shape

- large federal or defense vehicle
- visible prime
- partial public sub and award data
- likely incumbent continuity
- mission-specific customer and place of performance

### Expected strengths

AXIOM should prioritize:

- incumbent lineage
- visible sub ecosystem
- protest or legal path
- customer-map reconstruction
- labor and pricing analogs
- recompete posture

### Reject if

- the answer defaults to generic subscriptions as the main differentiator
- the answer leans on unavailable proposal data
- the answer does not separate observed facts from inferred capture posture
- the answer misses archive/diff, protest mining, or teammate inference

---

## Scenario 2: Thin-data mid-tier vendor

### Shape

- private contractor
- light corporate footprint
- sparse public control-path visibility
- modest contract residue
- unclear compliance posture

### Expected strengths

AXIOM should:

- avoid bluffing hidden control or exact pricing detail
- produce lawful next-step collection paths
- emphasize vendor assurance confidence ceilings
- identify what can be corroborated versus what stays weakly inferred
- use customer-provided uploads as optional confidence raisers, not assumptions

### Reject if

- the answer claims deep hidden-control intelligence from weak public traces alone
- the answer pretends dark space is solved
- the answer produces vehicle-heavy tactics with no vendor-assurance plan

---

## Scenario 3: High-noise recompete with sparse public sub data

### Shape

- recompete signal exists
- subaward visibility is incomplete
- multiple amendments or changing requirements
- visible market noise and weak direct teammate disclosure

### Expected strengths

AXIOM should prioritize:

- archive and diff of solicitations and amendments
- protest and court mining
- FOIA targeting
- customer-map reconstruction
- confidence-scored team inference

It should explicitly deprioritize:

- subscription sprawl
- weak media overlays
- speculative facility intelligence unless clearly relevant

### Reject if

- the answer relies mainly on generic open-source searches
- the answer treats sparse public sub data as a solved problem
- the answer cannot explain how Helios would turn partial evidence into a reusable collection asset

---

## Global Acceptance Criteria

AXIOM passes only if the answer:

- names one primary differentiator, not five
- clearly separates product spine from optional enrichment
- does not rely on protected or unavailable data
- explains why Helios can do this better than generic procurement databases
- produces tactics that are reusable and systematizable
- improves both vehicle intelligence and vendor assurance
- grades confidence honestly
- surfaces dark space rather than disguising it

AXIOM fails if the answer is mostly:

- product fantasy
- analyst folklore
- subscription shopping
- or unscalable cleverness
