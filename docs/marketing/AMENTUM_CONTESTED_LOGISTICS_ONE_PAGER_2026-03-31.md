# Helios for Amentum Contested Logistics

Date: March 31, 2026  
Audience: Amentum contracting, growth, sustainment, and mission-technology leaders

## What Helios Is

Helios is a mission-thread intelligence platform for contested sustainment.

It helps an operator answer four questions fast:

- Which suppliers and subsystems are brittle in this sustainment thread?
- Where are the hidden control, financing, and intermediary dependencies?
- Which nodes act like single points of failure?
- What is the next mitigation or evidence-collection move?

Helios is not a route-optimization or ERP replacement product.

It is the intelligence layer that helps Amentum understand whether a contested sustainment thread is trustworthy, resilient, and operationally explainable before a planning or contracting decision is made.

## Why This Matters For Amentum

Amentum's Honolulu Center for Contested Logistics is focused on planning, experimentation, and technology integration for Indo-Pacific sustainment under disruption.

That creates a practical decision problem:

- suppliers can look fine in isolation but fail inside a mission thread
- hidden parent, bank-route, service, or planning dependencies can create brittle concentration
- alternates are often assumed, not actually mapped
- contracting and mission teams need a fast way to see what breaks next

Helios is built to expose that operating picture.

## What Helios Shows

For one mission thread, Helios can produce:

- a thread-scoped graph of suppliers, subsystems, facilities, and key dependencies
- a ranked list of brittle members
- explicit control-path exposures
- unresolved evidence gaps
- recommended mitigations
- a member passport for the highest-risk node

The current seeded Amentum demo thread models:

- the Honolulu Center for Contested Logistics as the operating site
- a distributed sustainment orchestrator as the mission-critical subsystem
- a treasury route through a concentrated bank path
- an alternate lift provider
- a planning-orchestration maintainer acting like a single point of failure

## What A 30 To 45 Day Pilot Would Look Like

Scope:

- one contested sustainment mission thread
- `25-50` suppliers or support entities
- bounded user group
- unclassified and open-source first

Outputs:

- mission-thread briefing packet
- supplier and subsystem passports
- ranked brittle nodes
- alternate-coverage gaps
- weekly change monitoring

Success looks like:

- Amentum can explain a sustainment thread faster and with fewer blind spots
- contracting and mission teams can agree on the top brittle nodes
- Helios identifies real follow-up work, not just abstract graph activity

## The Right Wedge

The right first step is not “replace contested logistics systems.”

It is:

`Give Amentum a mission-thread intelligence layer that exposes brittle suppliers, hidden dependencies, and next mitigations before those issues become planning or sustainment failures.`
